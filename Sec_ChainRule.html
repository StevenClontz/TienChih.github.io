Section 7.10 Chain Rule

We've so far covered sums products and quotients of functions, so how about composition? The chain rule is: \(\frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]=f'(g(x))g'(x)\text{.}\) To see why:

\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]\amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h},\ \text{ Similar to the product rule, we will multiply by 1: }\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\textcolor{blue}{\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{g(x+h)-g(x)}}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\limit{h}{0}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h} \end{align*}

We're going tom play a second trick here, we are goin to let \(k=g(x+h)-g(x)\text{,}\) we also note then that \(g(x+h+=g(x)+k\text{.}\) Also note that when \(h\to 0\) It follows that \(k=g(x+h)-g(x)\to g(x+0)-g(x)=0\text{.}\) So we can make some replacements:

\begin{align*} \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\limit{h}{0}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{\textcolor{red}{k}}{0}\frac{f(\textcolor{red}{g(x)+k})-f(g(x))}{\textcolor{red}{k}}\limit{h}{0}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}\\ \amp =\amp f'(g(x))g'(x) \end{align*}

Subsection 7.10.1 Examples

Consider \(f(x)=\sqrt{x^2+1}\text{.}\) We can think of this as \(f(x)=g(h(x))\) where \(g(x)=\sqrt{x}=x^{1/2}, h(x)=x^2+1\text{.}\) So \(g'(x)=\frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}, h'(x)=2x\) and:

\begin{align*} f'(x)\amp =\amp \frac{d}{dx}[\sqrt{x^2+1}]\\ \amp =\amp \frac{d}{dx}[g(h(x))]\\ \amp =\amp g'(h(x))h'(x)\\ \amp =\amp \frac{1}{2}(x^2+1)^{-1/2}(2x)\\ \amp =\amp \frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2+1}}\text{.} \end{align*}

Another example, lets consider the derivative of \(y=(x^2+100)^{300}\text{.}\) We COULD expand this polynomial out, but lets agree no one wants that. On the other hand by the chain rule:

\begin{align*} \frac{dy}{dx}\amp =\amp \frac{d}{dx}[(x^2+100)^{300}],\ \text{ so by the chain rule: }\\ \amp =\amp 300(x^2+100)^{299}\frac{d}{dx}[x^2+100]\\ \amp =\amp 300(x^2+100)^{299}(2x)\\ \amp =\amp 600x(x^2+100)^{299}\text{.} \end{align*}
