We've so far covered sums products and quotients of functions, so how about composition? The chain rule is: \(\frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]=f'(g(x))g'(x)\text{.}\) To see why:

\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]\amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h},\ \text{ Similar to the product rule, we will multiply by 1: }\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\textcolor{blue}{\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{g(x+h)-g(x)}}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\limit{h}{0}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h} \end{align*}
