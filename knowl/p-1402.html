We should observe that if we have starting populations \(\begin{pmatrix} X_0 \\ Y_0\end{pmatrix}\) then in \(n\) years the respective populations will be:

\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} X_n \\ Y_n \end{pmatrix}= \begin{pmatrix} 0.6 \amp 0.2 \\ 0.4 \amp 0.8\end{pmatrix}^n\begin{pmatrix} X_0 \\ Y_0 \end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}

The best way to think of this is that applyng the matrix \(M= \begin{pmatrix} 0.6 \amp 0.2 \\ 0.4 \amp 0.8\end{pmatrix}\) shifts some of the value from \(X\) to \(Y\) and vice versa. Each we do this, we are simply reapplying \(M\text{.}\)
