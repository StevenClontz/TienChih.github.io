We should note, if \(x\) is the number of bags of food \(A\) and \(y\) the number of bags of food \(B\text{.}\) He is minimizing the cost \(C(x,y)=10x+12y\text{.}\) The inequalities we are subject to are:

\begin{align*} 40x+30y\amp \geq 150,\ \ \text{protein requirement}\\ 20x+20y\amp \geq 90,\ \ \text{mineral requirement}\\ 10x+30y\amp \geq 60,\ \ \text{vitamin requirement}\\ x\amp \geq 0,\ \ \text{ no negative bags }\\ y\amp \geq 0,\ \ \text{ no negative bags } \end{align*}

So sketching the inequalities, we obtain the following feasible region with corners: The profit at each corner is as follows:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c} x \amp y \amp C(x,y)\\ \hline 0 \amp 5 \amp 10(0)+12(5)=60\\ 1.5 \amp 3 \amp 10(1.5)+12(3)=51\\ 3.75 \amp 0.75 \amp 10(3.75)+12(0.75)=46.5\\ 6 \amp 0 \amp 10(6)+12(0)=60\end{array} \end{equation*}

So the cost is minimized when you purchase 3.75 bags of \(A\) and 0.75 bags of \(B\text{.}\)
