Let's say you take out a loan at $100 dollars and are charged 5% monthly simple interest. What this means is that every month, 5% of your initial debt is added on as interest. Over time you would see something like:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|} \hline \text{ Month } \amp \text{ Debt } \\ \hline 0 \amp 100\\ 1\amp 100+(.05)(100)=100+5=105\\ 2\amp 105+(.05)(100)=105+5=110\\ 3 \amp 110+(.05)(100)=110+5=115\\ \vdots \amp \vdots\\ n \amp 100+(.05)(100)n=100+5n\\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
