Consider \(f(x)=\sqrt{x^2+1}\text{.}\) We can think of this as \(f(x)=g(h(x))\) where \(g(x)=\sqrt{x}=x^{1/2}, h(x)=x^2+1\text{.}\) So \(g'(x)=\frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}, h'(x)=2x\) and:

\begin{align*} f'(x)\amp =\amp \frac{d}{dx}[\sqrt{x^2+1}]\\ \amp =\amp \frac{d}{dx}[g(h(x))]\\ \amp =\amp g'(h(x))h'(x)\\ \amp =\amp \frac{1}{2}(x^2+1)^{-1/2}(2x)\\ \amp =\amp \frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2+1}}\text{.} \end{align*} in-context
