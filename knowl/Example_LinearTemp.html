Example1.5.1

Question:  The freezing point of water is \(0^\circ\) Celsius and \(32^\circ\) Fahrenheit. The boiling point of water is \(100^\circ\) Celsius and \(212^\circ\) Fahrenheit.

  1. Find a linear function \(T(F)=C\) that converts Fahrenheit into Celsius.

  2. When it's 10 degrees Fahrenheit, what is the temperature in Celsius?

  3. When it's 30 degrees Celsius, what is the temperature in Fahrenheit?

Solution:  We are given that \(T(32)=0\) and \(T(212)=100\text{.}\) This gives us points \((32,0)\) and \((212,100)\text{,}\) so this bears some similarity to Example 1.3.3.

  1. We first identify the slope of the function:

    \begin{equation*} m=\frac{\Delta C}{\Delta F}=\frac{100-0}{212-32}=\frac{100}{180}=\TCR{\frac{5}{9}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

    Then choosing either point, say \(\TCB{(32,0)}\text{,}\) and point slope form, we have:

    \begin{align*} C-C_0\amp =\amp m(F-F_0)\\ C-\TCB{0}\amp =\amp \TCR{\frac{5}{9}}(F-\TCB{32})\\ C\amp =\amp \frac{5}{9}F-\frac{160}{9}\text{.} \end{align*}

    Thus \(T(F)=\frac{5}{9}F-\frac{160}{9}\)

  2. When it's 10 degrees, we have that \(\TCG{F=10}\) and so:

    \begin{align*} C\amp =\amp \frac{5}{9}F-\frac{160}{9}\\ C\amp =\amp \frac{5}{9}\cdot\TCG{10}-\frac{160}{9}\\ C\amp =\amp \frac{50}{9}-\frac{160}{9}\\ C\amp =\amp -\frac{110}{9}\approx-12.2222\text{.} \end{align*}

    So 10 degrees Fahrenheit is \(-\frac{110}{9}\approx-12.2222\) degrees Celsius.

  3. When it's 30 degrees Celsius i.e. \(\TCO{C=30}\) we have:

    \begin{align*} C\amp =\amp \frac{5}{9}F-\frac{160}{9}\\ \TCO{30}\amp =\amp \frac{5}{9}F-\frac{160}{9}\\ 30+\frac{160}{9}\amp =\amp \frac{5}{9}F\\ \frac{430}{9}\amp =\amp \frac{5}{9}F\\ F\amp =\amp \frac{430}{5}=86\text{.} \end{align*}

    So when it's 30 degrees Celsius, it is 86 degrees Fahrenheit.
