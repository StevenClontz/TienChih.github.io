Solution: 

  1. Since she is driving towards her mom's house, the distance from her to the house decreases at a rate of 75 miles per hour. Thus \(\TCR{m=-75}\text{.}\) At \(t=3\) hours, her distance was 200 miles away. Thus \(\TCB{(3,200)}\) falls on the line representing this function. Recall the techniques from Examples 1.3.2, Example 1.3.3.

    \begin{align*} D(t)\amp = -75t+b\\ \TCB{200}\amp = \TCR{-75}\cdot\TCB{3}+b\\ 200\amp = -225+b\\ b\amp = 200+225=425\text{.} \end{align*}

    Thus \(D(t)=-75t+425\text{.}\)

  2. At the start of the journey, \(\TCG{t=0}\text{,}\) and so she was \(D(\TCG{0})=-75\cdot\TCG{0}+425=425\) miles from her mother's house.

  3. When she arrives, her distance from her mothers house is \(\TCO{D(t)=0}\) miles and so:

    \begin{align*} D(t)\amp = -75t+425\\ \TCO{0}\amp = -75t+425\\ 75t\amp = 425\\ t\amp = \frac{425}{75}\approx 5.6667\text{.} \end{align*}

    So the trip takes \(t=\frac{425}{75}\approx 5.6667\) hours or 5 hours and 40 minutes.
