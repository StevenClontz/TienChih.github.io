Question:  Suppose you were selling widgets for $5 a unit, they have a marginal cost of $3 per unit, and a fixed cost of production of $20.

  1. Find the Cost, Revenue and Profit functions of producing \(x\) widgets (\(C(x), R(x), P(x)\)) in dollars.

  2. For each widget sold, how much does your profit increase?

  3. What is the cost of producing 30 widgets?

  4. What is the break even point? (Zero profit).

Solution: 

  1. We break these down one at a time.

    1. The marginal cost or cost per widget is $3, and the cost for producing no widgets is the fixed cost of $20. Thus \(C(x)=3x+20\text{.}\)

    2. You get $5 per widget sold, and clearly do not get any money for selling nothing, so \(R(x)=5x+0=5x\text{.}\)

    3. Profit is Revenue (money generated) minus Costs (money spent). Thus \(P(x)=R(x)-C(x)=5x-(3x+20)=2x-20\text{.}\)

  2. Since \(P(x)=2x-20\text{,}\) the change per unit of \(x\) or profit per widget is the slope, or $2/widget.

  3. When you produce \(\TCG{x=30}\) widgets, the cost will be:

    \begin{align*} C(x)\amp =\amp 3x+20\\ C(x)\amp =\amp 3\cdot\TCG{30}+20\\ C(x)\amp =\amp 90+20\\ C(x)\amp =\amp 110\text{.} \end{align*}

    The cost of producing 30 widgets is $110. \url{https://www.desmos.com/calculator/cmoor2agyv}

  4. The break even occurs when the profit is zero, \(\TCO{P(x)=0}\text{.}\)

    \begin{align*} P(x)\amp =\amp 2x-20\\ \TCO{0}\amp =\amp 2x-20\\ 2x\amp =\amp 20\\ x\amp =\amp 10\text{.} \end{align*}

    The break even point occurs when \(x=10\) widgets are sold. \url{https://www.desmos.com/calculator/bdtdeqzqqa}
