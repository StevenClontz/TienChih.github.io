An interesting byproduct of this is that if we were to solve \(ax^2+bx+c=0\text{,}\) we would get:

\begin{align*} ax^2+bx+c\amp = 0\\ a(x+\frac{b}{2a})^2+c-\frac{b^2}{4a}\amp = 0\\ a(x+\frac{b}{2a})^2\amp=\frac{b^2}{4a}-c\\ a(x+\frac{b}{2a})^2\amp=\frac{b^2-4ac}{4a}\\ (x+\frac{b}{2a})^2\amp=\frac{b^2-4ac}{4a^2}\\ x+\frac{b}{2a}\amp=\frac{\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\\ x\amp =\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a} \end{align*}
