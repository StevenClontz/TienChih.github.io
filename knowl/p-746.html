We note that a rectangle has length sides and two width sides. If \(\ell \) is the length of the length sides and \(w\) the width side, we obtain:

\begin{equation*} 2\ell+2w=200 \end{equation*}

or \(\ell+w=100\text{.}\) We also have that the area of a rectangle is \(A=\ell w\text{.}\) Thus:

\begin{align*} A\amp = \ell w\\ A\amp = (100-w)w\\ A\amp = -w^2+100w. \end{align*}

Thus the fenced region has area 0 when \(w=0,100\) the vertex must occur halfway in between at \(w=50, \ell=100-50=50, A=50\cdot 50=2500\) square feet.
