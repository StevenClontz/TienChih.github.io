Example1.5.3

Question:  Dr. Johnson is traveling to visit her mother for a holiday. She drives at a constant speed of 75 mph. After 3 hours, she passes a landmark that she knows is 200 miles from her mom's house.

  1. Find a function \(D(t)\) that gives Dr. Johnson's distance to her mother house, in miles, after \(t\) hours.

  2. How far away was she when she started driving?

  3. How many hours did it take from start to finish to complete this drive?

Solution: 

  1. Since she is driving towards her mom's house, the distance from her to the house decreases at a rate of 75 miles per hour. Thus \(\TCR{m=-75}\text{.}\) At \(t=3\) hours, her distance was 200 miles away. Thus \(\TCB{(3,200)}\) falls on the line representing this function. Recall the techniques from Examples 1.3.1, Example 1.3.2.

    \begin{align*} D(t)\amp =\amp -75t+b\\ \TCB{200}\amp =\amp \TCR{-75}\cdot\TCB{3}+b\\ 200\amp =\amp -225+b\\ b\amp =\amp 200+225=425\text{.} \end{align*}

    Thus \(D(t)=-75t+425\text{.}\) \url{https://www.desmos.com/calculator/uoul2cdsjs}

  2. At the start of the journey, \(\TCG{t=0}\text{,}\) and so she was \(D(\TCG{0})=-75\cdot\TCG{0}+425=425\) miles from her mother's house. \url{https://www.desmos.com/calculator/xuzh7xcgwk}

  3. When she arrives, her distance from her mothers house is \(\TCO{D(t)=0}\) miles and so:

    \begin{align*} D(t)\amp =\amp -75t+425\\ \TCO{0}\amp =\amp -75t+425\\ 75t\amp =\amp 425\\ t\amp =\amp \frac{425}{75}\approx 5.6667\text{.} \end{align*}

    So the trip takes \(t=\frac{425}{75}\approx 5.6667\) hours or 5 hours and 40 minutes. \url{https://www.desmos.com/calculator/dilrensiar}
