When it's 10 degrees, we have that \(\TCG{F=10}\) and so:

\begin{align*} C\amp = \frac{5}{9}F-\frac{160}{9}\\ C\amp = \frac{5}{9}\cdot\TCG{10}-\frac{160}{9}\\ C\amp = \frac{50}{9}-\frac{160}{9}\\ C\amp = -\frac{110}{9}\approx-12.2222\text{.} \end{align*}

So 10 degrees Fahrenheit is \(-\frac{110}{9}\approx-12.2222\) degrees Celsius.
