By the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra, we can write \(p(x)=a_nx^n+\cdots +a_0\) as:

\begin{equation*} p(x)=a_n(x-r_1)^{m_1}(x-r_2)^{m_2}\cdots (x-r_k)^{m_k}\cdot q_1(x)q_2(x)\cdots q_\ell(x) \end{equation*}

where the \(q_i(x)\) are irreducible quadratics. We call \(r_1, \ldots, r_k\) the roots of \(r(x)\text{,}\) where root \(r_i\) has multiplicty \(m_i\text{.}\) What this means is if we plug \(r_i\) into \(p(x)\text{,}\) we end up with 0, since that linear factor ends up being 0.
