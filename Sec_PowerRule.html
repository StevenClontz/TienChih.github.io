Section 7.5 Power Rule

We may have noticed a pattern with derivatives. But to illustrate it explicitly:

  • The derivative of \(f(x)=x^0\text{,}\) it's the derivative of the constant function \(f(x)=1\text{,}\) and since its a horizontal line, it never has any slope and \(f'(x)=0\text{.}\)

  • The derivative of \(f(x)=x^1\text{,}\) it's the derivative of the line \(f(x)=x\) which always has slope 1, so \(f'(x)=1\text{.}\)

  • The derivative of \(f(x)=x^2\) is:

    \begin{align*} f(x)\amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0} \frac{(x+h)^2-x^2}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0} \frac{x^2+2xh+h^2-x^2}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0} \frac{(2x+h)h}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0} 2x+h=2x\text{.} \end{align*}

  • The derivative of \(f(x)=x^3\) is:

    \begin{align*} f(x)\amp =\amp \limit{h}{0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0} \frac{(x+h)^3-x^3}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0} \frac{x^3+3x^2h+3xh^2+h^3-x^3}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0} \frac{(3x^2+3xh+h^2)h}{h}\\ \amp =\amp \limit{h}{0} 3x^2+3xh+h^2=3x^2\text{.} \end{align*}

To generalize this, we have a rule of differentiation called the power rule which goes: \(\frac{d}{dx}[x^n]=nx^{n-1}\text{.}\)

To verify:

  • \(\frac{d}{dx}[x^0]=0x^{0-1}=0\text{.}\)

  • \(\frac{d}{dx}[x^1]=1x^{1-1}=1\text{.}\)

  • \(\frac{d}{dx}[x^2]=2x^{2-1}=2x\text{.}\)

  • \(\frac{d}{dx}[x^3]=3x^{3-1}=3x^2\text{.}\)

What's fascinating is that this rule applies to all powers, not just integer powers.

Subsection 7.5.1 Examples

We can combine these rules to find the derivatives of some basic functions. So consider the derivative of \(f(x)=x^4-2x^2+7x-3\text{.}\) It would be:

\begin{align*} f'(x)\amp =\amp \frac{d}{dx}[x^4-2x^2+7x-3]\\ \amp =\amp \frac{d}{dx}[x^4-2x^2+7x^1-3x^0]\\ \amp =\amp 4x^3-2(2x^1)+7(1x^0)-3(0x^{-1})\\ \amp =\amp 12x^3-4x+7\text{.} \end{align*}

Whereas the derivative of \(g(x)=\sqrt{4x}+\frac{3}{x}-\frac{2}{x^2}\) would be:

\begin{align*} g'(x)\amp =\amp \frac{d}{dx}[\sqrt{4x}+\frac{3}{x}-\frac{2}{x^2}]\\ \amp =\amp \frac{d}{dx}[2x^{1/2}+3x^{-1}-2x^{-2}]\\ \amp =\amp 2(1/2)x^{-1/2}+3(-1x^{-2})-2(2x^{-3})\\ \amp =\amp x^{-1/2}-3x^{-2}+4x^{-3}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}-\frac{3}{x^2}+\frac{4}{x^3}\text{.} \end{align*}
